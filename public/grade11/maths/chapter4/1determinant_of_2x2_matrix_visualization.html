📚 what you learn
• Determinant formula: det = ad − bc (cross-multiply main diagonal minus anti-diagonal).
• Geometric meaning: |det| equals the area of the parallelogram formed by the column vectors (a,c) and (b,d).
• Zero determinant: means the vectors are parallel (collinear) — the parallelogram collapses to a line (area = 0).
• Sign: positive det means vectors are oriented counter‑clockwise; negative means clockwise.
