Lesson Notes

In the Thomson Model, the atom is a "pudding" of positive charge. Alpha particles should pass straight through with zero deflection.

Deflections (>10°): 0

What I Learned

  • Nucleus: Atoms have a tiny, dense center.
  • Empty Space: Most particles pass through because atoms are mostly void.
  • Repulsion: Positive alphas bounce off positive nuclei.
