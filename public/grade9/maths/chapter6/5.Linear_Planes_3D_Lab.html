A linear equation in three variables (x, y, z) represents a plane in 3D space — not a line! This is fundamental for multivariable calculus, physics, and engineering.
⚡ The plane is defined by a·x + b·y + c·z = 0. Changing coefficients tilts the plane in different directions.
